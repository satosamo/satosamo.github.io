## The Schrödinger equation

For the Coulomb potential the time-independent Schrödinger equation reads

$$\left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu}\nabla^2 - \frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r}\right]\psi = E\psi.$$

Separating $\psi = R(r)\,Y_\ell^m(\theta, \varphi)$ produces the quantum numbers $n, \ell, m$, with energies

$$E_n = -\frac{13.6\ \text{eV}}{n^2},$$

independent of $\ell$ — an accidental degeneracy specific to the $1/r$ potential.
